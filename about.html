About Larnix

Why Larnix exists

Artificial intelligence is reshaping how we work, learn, and create — yet for most people it stays a black box, gated behind expensive courses, pricey hardware, and walls of jargon. We think that is backwards. Understanding the technology shaping your future should not depend on your budget or your laptop.

So Larnix is free, open, and built on one belief: the fastest way to understand something is to build it. Not to watch someone else build it — to build it yourself, line by line, until it clicks.

How you’ll learn

  • Build first, theory as you go. Every chapter has you run real code and see a real result, then explains why it works. You learn by doing, the way you actually remember.
  • One idea at a time. Short, single-topic chapters in plain language. New terms are introduced in everyday words before the technical name appears.
  • It runs where you are. Beginner chapters execute Python right in your browser, with nothing to install. The GPU-heavy modules run on free cloud tiers, and any lesson that touches a paid service shows a free alternative.
  • Zero to frontier. Seventeen modules take you from “what is AI” through classical machine learning, deep learning, transformers, and large language models — ending with you pretraining your own small LLM.

What you’ll walk away with

The goal is not a certificate you forget. It is the genuine ability to read, build, and reason about modern AI systems — to train and evaluate models honestly, to explain how a transformer works because you wrote one, and to ship a project you are proud to show. In short: from curious beginner to someone who can build.

Where it is today

Larnix is being built in the open, and it is early. Right now the platform is complete and proven end to end on the first full chapter — Train your first model, in your browser — and new modules are being authored from there. You can start that chapter today, for free; follow the roadmap to see what is coming next.

If that mission speaks to you, the best first step is the smallest one: train your first model.
